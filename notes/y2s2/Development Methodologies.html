Development Methodologies

The Waterfall Model

The OG

Characteristics

  • Based on general engineering princples
  • Each phase is defined by what is created at that stage
  • Phases are natural milestones, a change in emphasis

Advantages

  • Simple to understand
  • Development process is easier to plan, manage and follow
  • Overall design is unlikely to change (thus above)

Disadvantages

  • Rigid process, inflexible
  • Responding to change is harder, must go through previous stages
  • User is less likely to get exactly the solution they want (see above)

Incremental development

Essentially a variation of the waterfall model

Characteristics

  • Get working software early in the cycle
  • Build on existing working software, adding new functionality with each increment

Advantages

  • Extra milestones
  • Problems are usually found earlier in the process

Disadvantages

  • A strong design is necessary, and a good understand of dependencies between milestones

Evolutionary Prototyping

Basically hacking it together; Suitable as part of a larger methodology

Characteristics

  • Each evolution adds functionality until the code meets all the requirements
  • Kind of an excuse for not having a methodology, gets messy quickly

Advantages

  • Useful when the exact requirements are difficult to explain or quantify
  • Very fast (and loose)

Disadvantages

  • Not stable, doesn't have a specified design.
  • Heavy refactoring needed as part of a larger method

Boehm's Spiral

This is barely ever used, but it's still very influential; Sometimes described as a meta-model

Characteristics

  • Used when the requirements are not well defined
  • Primarily reduces risk, through working in 'loops', increasing effort per loop
  • If the project fails/is unfeasible/encounters problems, this approach ensures the minimal time, money and effort is lost
  • Four tasks per 'loop':
    • Set an objective for the loop
    • Evaluate alternatives, identify risk
    • Develop and validate
    • Plan the next loop (if/how to continue)

An Example

A company wants to make a self driving car. They use the spiral model:

  • Can we make a car that drives itself?
  • Can we make a car that drives on a closed straight of road?
  • Can we make a car that drives on a motorway?
  • Can we make a car that drives on twisty roads?
  • Can we make a car that drives in the city?

Each loop builds on the last, and effectively includes a whole waterfall (or other methodology) within it.

Advantages

  • Flexible
  • Very low risk, cost effective
  • Emphasis on client approval/project feasability

Disadvantages

  • Infinite spirals can be costly and vast
  • Not ideal for low-risk or small projects
  • Strong risk analysis is key

Agile Methodology

A reaction to the repeated failure of large plan-based projects, and the inertia and that comes with software development

Types of Agile

Lots of agile methodologies exist:

  • XP
  • SCRUM
  • RAD
  • DSDM
  • Crystal
  • Agile Unified Process

The Four Agile Values

  • Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  • Working software over comprehensive documentation
  • Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  • Responding to change over following a plan

There is value to be had in the right-hand items, but the left items are more important.

The 12 Agile Principles

  1. Customer satisfaction by early and continuous delivery of useful software
  2. Welcome changing requirements, even late in development
  3. Working software is delivered frequently (weeks rather than months)
  4. Close, daily cooperation between business people and developers
  5. Projects are built around motivated individuals, who should be trusted
  6. Face-to-face conversation is the best form of communication (co-location)
  7. Working software is the principal measure of progress
  8. Sustainable development, able to maintain a constant pace
  9. Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design
  10. Simplicity - the art of maximizing the amount of work not done - is essential
  11. Self-organizing teams
  12. Regular adaptation to changing circumstance
