Project Management

Role of a PM

Project management is the art of balancing time, cost, quality and scope of a project.

They must:

  • Plan the project
    • Create a project plan and other management documents e.g.
      • Quality plan (QA)
      • Validation plan (testing)
      • Maintenance plan (maintenance)
  • Calculate risk
  • Monitor the project
  • Control the project and solve problems that arise

The two most difficult parts of a PM's job are the work breakdown and project schedule. These involve deciding on activities, the dependencies and time schedule of those activities, then deciding on milestones and deliverables and allocating resources appropriately. Remember the plan and to-do lists? Work breakdown + schedule.

Project Monitoring

The PM should constantly be checking the progress of the project against the plan. The PM can assess if there's potential for setbacks or slippage, and take action if there is a problem. Major changes made should be reflected in the project plan.

Possible responses problems in the project

  • Swapping staff around
  • Renegotiating the scope
  • Revising the plan
  • Improve resources available to the team
  • Deal with under-performing staff
