Process Improvement

There is a direct link between process quality and product quality for manufacturing. The Toyota Way demonstrates this clearly. Software engineering can draw many similarities, given software quality is also dependent on staff skills and experience, budget, time, people, technology etc. Process Improvement is the decomposition of existing processes, measuring their decomposed aspects and seeking to improve those aspects.

Choose a metric that represents the time and resource to complete the process. e.g.

  • Average defects found in a given review
  • Branches of code
  • Time to perform a task

SEI CMM

SEI CMM is the Software Engineering Institute's Capability-Maturity Model. Originally made to stop the US DoD getting ripped off, it has developed into a framework that a team's processes can be measured against. It is staged as five levels.

The framework itself is very prescriptive and improvement-oriented, compared to ISO-9000

  • Level 1: Initial
    • Inexperienced software companies
    • Unstable, ad-hoc process
    • Project success relies heavily on staff skills and experience
  • Level 2: Repeatable
    • Able to repeat earlier success
    • Stable project planning
    • Tracking of important metrics
    • Project standards
  • Level 3: Defined
    • A stable but non-improving state
    • Stable process model
    • Full process training program
    • Entry/exit criteria (The requirements to be met before starting/before finishing a process)
  • Level 4: Managed
    • Management by measurement
    • Quantitative targets
    • Productivity and quality measures
  • Level 5: Optimising
    • Commitment to continuous process improvement
    • Defect prevention
