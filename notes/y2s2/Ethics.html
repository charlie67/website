Making the right decisions in difficult situations

Basis for ethical decisions

Ethical basis can come from

  • Experiences
  • Opinions
  • Beliefs
  • Principles
  • Guidelines
  • Laws

Not just because "it's right or it's wrong", nor on cost or benefit.

It's a good idea to have general principles on where you stand. What are your thoughts on?..

  • Military work
  • Privacy and security
  • Liberty and censorship
  • Environmental issues
  • Exploitation of others
  • Workers rights
  • Lying, theft, murder (crime)

Applying an ethical code

  • Clarify the situation and issues
  • Consider all the viewpoints
  • Respect the views of others, and your own
  • Take responsibility

Tests for ethical behaviour

  • Gut feeling
  • Need to keep quiet
  • Could I tell my Mum
  • TV Interview
  • If it was done to me

BCS Code of Conduct

Members of the BCS are required to adhere to the code of conduct. It describes the things its members must do, covering four ethical aspects of the field. A short rundown is as follows:

Public interest

  • Have due regard public health, privacy, security and well-being
  • Have due regard for the legitimate rights of third parties
  • Conduct professional actives without discrimination
  • Promote equal access to the benefits of IT and promote inclusion of all sectors where opportunities arise

Professional Competence and Integrity

  • Only work within your professional competence
  • Keep up to date in your field
  • Comply with relevant legislation
  • Respect alternative viewpoints and exchange honest criticisms of work
  • Abstain from bribery or unethical inducement

Duty to Relevant Authority (Employer usually)

  • Carry out responsibilities with due care whilst exercising judgement
  • Avoid conflicts of interest between you and your relevant authority
  • Accept responsibility for your work and the work of those beneath you
  • Do not disclose confidential information
  • Do not misrepresent information (unless lawfully bound [i.e. NDA])

Duty to the Profession

  • Uphold the profession's reputation, the BCS' reputation and your own reputation
  • Keep up to date in your field
  • Act with integrity in professional circumstances
  • Notify the BCS upon becoming bankrupt or convicted of a criminal offence
