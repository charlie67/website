Quality Assurance and Review

Definition: Ensuring the software quality is meeting the requirements of the user (and other shareholders)

Quality factors (as defined by ISO 9126:1992)

  • Functionality
    • Satisfying stated or implied needs of the software's function
  • Reliability
    • Maintain performance under stated conditions for stated period of time
  • Usability
    • The effort needed for use by a stated or implied set of users
  • Efficiency
    • The relationship between performance and resources
  • Maintainability
    • The effort needed to make modifications
  • Portability
    • The ability of software to be transferred from one environment to another

Getting good software

Quality assurance is where the software development process is managed to ensure quality. It is a set of procedures and processes that make sure software is built the right way. There are general princples but they will be specialised for companies and projects.

The ISO 9000 Family

  • ISO 9000
    • Document family guidelines
  • ISO 9001
    • Model for quality assurance in design, development, production, installation and servicing
  • ISO 9002
    • Model for QA in production, installation and servicing
  • ISO 9003
    • Model for QA in final inspection and test
  • ISO 9004
    • Guidelines on quality management and quality system elements
  • ISO 9000-3
    • Application of ISO 9001 to the development, supply and maintenance of software
    • There is a direct correspondence between clauses of ISO 9001 and ISO 9000-3

Examples of sections from ISO 9000-3 include:

  • Management responsibilities
  • Testing and validation
  • Document control
  • Corrective action

ISO 9001 vs ISO 9000-3

ISO 9001

...Quality planning shall be consistent with all other requirements of a supplier’s quality system and shall be documented in a format to suit the supplier's method of operation. The supplier shall give consideration to...

ISO 9000-3

Quality planning should address…

  • Quality requirements, expressed in measurable terms, where appropriate;
  • The life cycle model to be used for software development
  • Defined criteria for starting and ending each project phase
  • Identification of types of reviews, tests and other verification and validation activities to be carried out
  • Identification of configuration management processes to be carried out

Process in a nutshell

  1. Define what is 'good quality'
  2. Define a procedure for verifying this
  3. Carry out said procedure
  4. Record the result
  5. Take and record and corrective action

Reviews

Reviews involve documentation or code being inspected by a group with the aim of finding any bugs or problems. It is usually conducted as a meeting and may involve the writer, QA, PL and others. Reviews are the best way to find bugs.

Documents needed for review

  • The item under review
  • The documents from which the item is derived
    • i.e. For test spec, requirements, UI design, maybe program design
    • i.e. For code, design document
  • The relevant quality plan documents (e.g. SE.QA.__)
  • A review checklist that summaries what major issues to look for in a quick and easy way

QA Summary

  • QA is the process of doing things in such a way as to ensure that end results meet the users requirements.
  • Steps must be appropriate & economically realistic.
  • Aim is not highest possible quality, but is a compromise between – Time – Cost – Quality – Scope
